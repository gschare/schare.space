2. The Train

Employee handbook says it is best to take the train to the office. Studies show that workplace performance is highest when you board the last car on an elevated track and watch the wake the rails make. Don’t resist the bouncing of the train.

You take the 9:42 Shady Grove bound Red Line—you know, the one that forms a U—transfer to the Green Line towards Greenbelt (apt) at Fort Totten, get off at College Park U of Md and wait for the bus at the station and then another bus to LPS right next to the 4H; get off on the side of the road once you’re on Greenmead.

What did you read on the way? Was it The Atlantic? You can do better than that.

3. Comedy

  • Lampoon the office
  • It is the only way to acknowledge the central role it plays in your life
    • When Dilbert burns down the office, the spectacle is comparable to blowing up the Death Star or Beaufort
      • The office itself, not the manager, is the villain
      • A building is a character
  • Comedy elevates our own experience in life to melodrama which more closely approximates our emotional subjectivity
    • Comedy is more dramatic than drama
  • Someone’s got a case of the Mondays
  • Are you brave enough to tell your therapist how much you love going to the office?
  • How many more offices do you hope to live in throughout your life?
  • You say so much with a space.
