Easing into Gaming

Jun 27, 2024. Last updated Jul 22, 2024.

A familiar picture: a man (a “nerdy” man), late teens or early 20s, grew up playing video games on his computer. He’s tech savvy. He’s intellectually sophisticated enough to consider video games an art form—or if not that, then a worthwhile medium of entertainment. Video games, he thinks, obey the same rules as movies: most content released is drivel, but a precious few are genuine cultural milestones, worthy of consideration in the high halls of society’s artistic achievements.

Are you this man?
Do you have a girlfriend?
Do you want her to appreciate video games the way you do?

If you answered yes to all of these questions, look no further: what follows is a list of video games with which to introduce your girlfriend to the fineness of the medium, at a comfortable pace which feels fun and collaborative.

Baba Is You

The problem is introducing her to video games gently (i.e., simple controls, easy to understand, few assumptions of general gaming knowledge) while providing a sufficiently engaging experience that she is convinced of the worth of video games. The solution is Baba Is You.

Minecraft

As a 3D game, Minecraft’s controls are difficult. You may consider playing with a gamepad rather than a mouse and keyboard. I’ve found it can be slow to get acquainted with the mouse and keyboard as a control scheme for 3D video games. However, Minecraft is wildly appealing and fundamentally cooperative and creative. Turn on peaceful mode or creative mode and build something together.

Fushimi Inari

A short pure walking game with 3D controls and a relaxed atmosphere, Fushimi Inari is ideal for getting acquainted with 3D games in an unstressful setting.

Terraria

Terraria is more difficult than Minecraft—not in terms of controls, but in gameplay challenges. Be patient and forgiving, and don’t take it too seriously. Do not expect to finish it. Just build a house.

Portal 2

Cooperative puzzle game—suitable almost from the start, by design, if using a gamepad.

Civilization V

Introduction to turn-based strategy and “map games”. Relaxed but complex. Play against the computer, or perhaps each other, with a long turn timer.

Hollow Knight

A challenging platformer with appealing gameplay and visuals and an expansive world. Install the multiplayer mod and play together on LAN. Take your time.

Skyrim

Once she is comfortable enough with 3D games a bit of thrilling action, show her Skyrim and explore a vast, immersive world. Read all the books. Treat it like anthropology: with no instructions, where does she go? How does she engage? A person’s first RPG experience is something to cherish, as too often those of us familiar with the genre carry certain expectations and play how we’ve learned to play, having lost the sense of wonder and openness inherent to such games. SkyUI is a must.

Red Dead Redemption? II?

I have not tried this, but I bet if she can handle Skyrim she can handle RDR. Story-rich and similarly expansive and immersive, she might enjoy one of the games lauded for masterful narrative and engaging characters.

Disco Elysium

She is a communist and she passes every real-life CONCEPTUALIZATION check. This game has two buttons (left click and right click) and instead of encounters involving real-time combat, they mostly feature tactical conversation, like a Bene Gesserit. There has never been anything more perfect for her.
