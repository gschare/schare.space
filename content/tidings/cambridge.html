Choosing Season

Sep 16, 2024 Choosing Season
  • There is a short season just before fall but after summer,
    • where people wear long or short sleeves,
    • and it is still light out late,
    • and the plants are still thriving
    • (though there are hints of yellowed leaves on the ground);
    • all greens, blues, and greys.
  • You eat outside in the sun or the shade,
    • regulated by an easy breeze.
  • The nights are cool but not cold.
  • This anonymous season enraptures the mind,
    • promising possibility, and lasting,
    • though in fact much briefer than all other seasons,
    • seemingly as long as attention permits.
  • Fleeting as it is, the season of choices,
    • when it goes, goes quickly.
  • Take too long to choose
    • (short sleeve or not, inside or out, love or flirt)
    • and you will be denied its gift:
    • to be varied and floating, curious
    • before the ground gets hard.

  • Following the season of choice is the season of instruction.
  • Where rapid change submits to being led,
    • deferral to the experts in the shadow of sure missteps.
  • Where the committal ones grit their teeth
    • in regret of the choices they made,
    • and others scramble to secure a place in the early sunset of scarcity.
  • Here is the peril of knowing too much,
    • where expectation comes into contact with reality.
  • Cower, waver if you must, but the fault is your own.
  • You should have known better.
  • You could have done differently.
  • Mistake after mistake makes the minutes of your autumn complete.

  • Between these two seasons lies the chasm of missed opportunity.
  • Go on, confront the question:
    • Rush back and take the path untaken,
      • hoping for greener grass as the grass dies under a white tarp?
    • Or push forth, uncertain and unhappy,
      • recite your lines,
      • expecting your thermic energy to burn away the chaff
      • and find the substance in structure?
    • Or, third, reject forced choices and commit to uncommitting,
      • the easiest way out and the hardest path to follow:
      • to choose nothing after all?

  • It is a script we all follow, hardwired and inevitable.
  • Frustrating, the process of falling, for the exit sign is illuminated
    • and no path is identity but one contains it;
    • if only space was discernible,
    • maybe these lines would lead to the origin.
  • Another thing to do, another assignment to hand it,
    • another lecture to follow, this script lacks imagination.
  • Made still and struck blind,
    • it has become impossible to read the signs.

  • Rest easy knowing at any time you may run away,
    • escape, rushing off into familiar darkness
    • out of preference for the absence of experience,
    • knowing nothing alive is there to witness your flight
    • except for the ear of the infinite, still, and silent night.
