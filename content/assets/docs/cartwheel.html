Cartwheel Handbook.

Food

  • Buy canned wet food from any market. Brands e.g. Friskies, 9Lives, Tastefuls.
    • Give him ~2 5oz cans a day. Lately he has been eating less.
  • Optionally buy kibble/dry food and treats.
  • He won’t eat more than he needs, so don’t worry about overfeeding him.

Water

  • Keep a small bowl of water out. I find he doesn’t often drink water since the wet food contains enough water for him, but sometimes he does.

Litter

  • Buy any typical bodega litter.
  • Scoop poop daily.
  • Change litter and clean box weekly.

Behavior

  • He likes to chew cables when he’s understimulated. Just pick him up and move him away when he does this, or play with him.
  • He also chews cables, plants, paper and cardboard because it’s good for his teeth. I have a plant I’ve basically sacrificed to him, and I let him chew on newspapers and cardboard boxes.
  • He likes boxes he can sit in.
  • His carrier is a safe space for him. Leave it out and the door open so he can go there whenever he wants.

Rules

  • Don’t take him outside, at all, ever.
  • Don’t let him eat food except for his cat food unless you have checked that it’s safe for cats to eat.
    • Common misconception: cats can’t have dairy. They also can’t have chocolate, and many fruits are toxic to them, such as grapes and citrus.
    • That being said it is a nice treat to occasionally give him some delicious and safe human food.
