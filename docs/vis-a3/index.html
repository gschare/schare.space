Reading and Critique

Insights about the data:

  • Hard to identify consistent patterns from less than 2 years of data. Winter is of course a spike in cases (this is standard for disease transmission, with everyone cooped up inside) but US Covid cases only started appearing after the peak of winter in 2020, so there’s only a year of data.
  • Early summer is indeed “less favorable for viral transmission” but early fall 2021 was much worse than 2020. This might have to do with people returning to schools after summer break. Maybe 2021 was worse than 2020 because there were less precautions taken to mitigate transmission.
  • If 2022 is similar to 2021, we should expect cases to decline starting in mid-January. And like 2021 and 2020, they might plateau from the end of February until a further decline in summer, starting in May and reaching a minimum in June.
